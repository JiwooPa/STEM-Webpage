STEM Project: The Flame Change Scroll Buttons We learned that different chemical substances can make different colored flames when they are ignited. This phenomenon happens because of different type of metal and salt in the substance. When the metal atoms get hot, they make light, and that light makes the flame look a specific color. For example: Sodium mixture produces a yellow flame, Copper mixture makes a blue-green flame, Strontium mixture makes a red flame, and Potassium mixture makes a purple flame. This happens because of something called a line spectrum. When the atoms get hot, their electrons get excited and get to a higher energy level. When they comes back down, they emit energy as light. Each metal has its own pattern, so each substances makes a different color. Also, if two substances have the same chemical substances included, they will make the same flame color.
